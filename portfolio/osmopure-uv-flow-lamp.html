Overview

While at OsmoPure Technologies, I took the initiative to translate project–level mechanical requirements into a machinable CAD design for a leak-proof UV flow cell. The housing encases a central UV lamp, with the flow-path thickness precisely defined by the spacing between the lamp and the interior wall. The design leverages the limited penetration depth of UV light (per the Beer–Lambert law)—thinner flow paths increase per-cycle volatile TOC removal during post-purification UV polishing of pressure-distillation permeate streams. Real-world constraints were explicitly incorporated, including O-ring sizing, standard availability, sealing geometry, and manufacturability, resulting in a robust, serviceable design suitable for lab and pilot-scale use.

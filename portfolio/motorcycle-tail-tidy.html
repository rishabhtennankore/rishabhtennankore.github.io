Overview

I designed a minimal, street-legal tail tidy for the Yamaha XSR700 to clean up the rear profile for a bobber-inspired build. The custom housing integrates the tail lamp, turn signals, and license plate mount into a single compact assembly while retaining OEM mounting points and proper lighting geometry. The design was developed as a fully parametric CAD model and prepared for 3D printing using Formlabs' Rigid 10K Resin, chosen for its exceptional tensile and flexural strength, and heat resistance. Key design considerations included structural stiffness, wire routing, and real-world packaging constraints. This project blended aesthetic-driven design with practical engineering and materials selection.
